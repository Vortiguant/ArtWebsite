About Sunita

Documenting sunlight through pigment, textiles, and layered washes for collectors who crave warmth and tactility.

Biography

Raised in Mumbai and trained in textile design, Sunita Kamal built her practice translating sensory memories of light into layered artworks. After residencies in Lisbon and Vancouver, she founded the Instagram journal Art and sunny things to document experiments in natural dyes, clay illustration, and plein-air sketching.

Her collectors include boutique hotels across India, creative directors in Europe, and families who want art that keeps their spaces optimistic. Sunita now calls Abu Dhabi home while maintaining pop-up studios abroad, building collections that pair luminous color fields with hand-drawn botanicals.

Artist Statement

I paint and sculpt to preserve the fleeting glow that hits courtyards, rooftops, and lakes at dawn. Every piece begins with field recordings—swatches of sari borders, pressed petals, snippets of poetry—which I translate into gestural marks, embroidered textures, or carved clay lines. My palettes lean on turmeric, kumkum, and terracotta hues so viewers feel transported to sun-soaked climates.

Whether I am hand-lettering holiday cards or shaping koi-inspired trays, I want the work to feel like portable warmth: tactile, kind, and full of stories.

Studio Notebook

In 2024 I committed to slower, research-led releases. I am cataloging light shifts across Abu Dhabi rooftops and mangrove inlets each month, mapping them into topographic paintings, and pairing the pieces with ceramic keepsakes so collectors can hold the palette in their hands.
